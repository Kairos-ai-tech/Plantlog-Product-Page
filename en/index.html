01 Ritual The core loop Four moves, forever

A loop that rhymes,
not a task list that nags. Not reminders — a rhythm that comes back around.

You repeat four moves. Snap one, log one, get the push, check Today. Each is polished short enough to live in your day — not to be filed under "later."

I01

Add a plantSnap & identify

Take a photo. The AI returns species candidates from Plant.id with care difficulty, light, and watering preference. Preview appears on the add sheet instantly; upload failures now surface loudly.

II02

Log observationsStructured journalling

Watering, fertilizing, repotting, pruning, issues, milestones — seven structured event types. Measure leaves, log soil moisture, give a 1–5 health score, up to 4 photos per entry.

III03

Receive remindersScheduled push

Server-scheduled push notifications, delivered reliably. Tapping opens the plant directly; the "Done" action sits right in the notification — no app open required.

IV04

Today screenCross-plant view

Overdue, today, and upcoming across every plant — three aggregated groups. The home card only appears when there's actual work to do.

04 Oracle The AI inside One photo, real work

Your first photo
does the first job. Let the first photo deliver the first real result.

An empty "no plants yet" screen is why most tools lose users. PlantLog makes the first photo earn a real species ID and a health diagnosis — feedback in the first session, so you don't leave.

i
Identify. Species candidates + confidence score, with care difficulty, watering preference, and light level.
ii
Diagnose. Yellowing, overwatering, pests — photo → symptom list → an actionable care prescription.
iii
Quota UX. Usage isn't a threat; the AI tab carries a live usage chip, and hitting your limit surfaces a paywall with current and monthly allowances spelled out clearly.
